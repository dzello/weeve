About

weeve is a tiny open source experiment made during re-allocated winter break family time by @dzello.

Developers, learn more about weeve - a server-less application - on Github at dzello/weeve.
How's it work?

Anyone who finds this page can join the weeve by signing in with their Twitter account. Once a user has signed in, their Twitter user stream will be interleaved into the stream of tweets on the page. Everybody gets to watch everyone else's user stream.

What's a user stream?

Your user stream is not the tweets you post. Rather, it's your feed. It's the stream of tweets from everyone you follow.

What happens when I leave the page or close my browser?

You leave the weeve, and your stream stops going to the weeve. But you can always come back, as long as the weeve isn't full.

What's a weeve like compared to normal Twitter?

It's like if you only followed the users in the weeve, but they were retweeting every single tweet from their stream.

Will I find this useful?

Well, that's up to you! But ideally by participating in a weeve, or just watching one, you'll find new and interesting things on Twitter you didn't know before.

Note: If you're uncomfortable with other users seeing your Twitter feed, please do not use this site. If you follow users with protected tweets, get their permission before joining a weeve.
What should I do next?

Close this modal window in 5 seconds. Then click 'Sign in with Twitter' (weeve requires only read access). When you come back, you'll see your avatar in the row of users and your stream will begin to appear like magic.
